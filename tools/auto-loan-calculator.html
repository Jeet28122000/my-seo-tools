Loan Details

What is an Auto Loan?

An auto loan is a type of secured loan that allows individuals to purchase a vehicle by paying in monthly installments over a fixed period. The vehicle itself acts as collateral for the loan.

Instead of paying the full price upfront, borrowers can spread the cost over time, making it easier to afford expensive vehicles.

  • Principal: Total loan amount
  • Interest Rate: Cost of borrowing
  • Loan Tenure: Duration of repayment

Auto Loan EMI Formula

Our calculator uses the standard mathematical formula:

EMI = [P × R × (1 + R)^N] / [(1 + R)^N – 1]

  • P: Loan amount (Principal)
  • R: Monthly interest rate (Annual Rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
  • N: Number of monthly installments (Years × 12)

Example of Car Loan Calculation

  • Loan Amount: ₹5,00,000
  • Interest Rate: 10% annually
  • Tenure: 5 years

Using the formula, your monthly EMI would be approximately ₹10,624. This helps you understand affordability before applying for a loan.

Factors Affecting Car Loan EMI

  • Loan Amount: Higher amount increases EMI
  • Interest Rate: Higher rate means higher EMI
  • Loan Tenure: Longer tenure reduces EMI but increases total interest
  • Credit Score: Better score gets lower interest rates

Tips to Reduce Your Car Loan EMI

  • Increase down payment
  • Choose shorter tenure if affordable
  • Compare lenders before choosing
  • Maintain a good credit score
  • Negotiate interest rates

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is a good interest rate for a car loan?

Typically between 7% to 12% depending on credit score and lender.

Can I prepay my car loan?

Yes, most lenders allow prepayment but may charge a small fee.

Does EMI remain fixed?

Yes, for fixed-rate loans EMI remains constant throughout the tenure.

How much loan can I afford?

It depends on your income, expenses, and existing liabilities.
