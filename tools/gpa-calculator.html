What is GPA?

GPA (Grade Point Average) is a numerical representation of a student's academic performance. It is calculated by dividing total grade points by total credits.

How to Calculate GPA

Each course is assigned a grade and credit value. Multiply grade points by credits, sum them, and divide by total credits.

Grade Scale

  • A+ = 4.3
  • A = 4.0
  • A- = 3.7
  • B+ = 3.3
  • B = 3.0
  • B- = 2.7
  • C+ = 2.3
  • C = 2.0
  • C- = 1.7
  • D = 1.0
  • F = 0.0

Why GPA Matters

GPA is important for college admissions, scholarships, and job opportunities. Maintaining a high GPA can open many academic and career paths.
